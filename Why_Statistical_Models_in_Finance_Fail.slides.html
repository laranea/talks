Covariance Estimation¶

A common problem in portfolio optimization is to calculate a covariance matrix and use it to do some math.

In order to have a stable covariance matrix estimate, you need $T > N$, where $N$ is your observation count and $N$ is your number of assets.

Stationarity¶

A property of well-behaved time series

Stationarity implies that all segments of a time series appear to be drawn from the same probability distribution

This is a key assumption underlying most statistical models

Overfitting¶

Overfitting is when you fit to the noise in a set of observations rather than a signal

This is a problem that you run into in all applications of statistics, finance is just particularly noisy
